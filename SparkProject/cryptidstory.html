Famous Accounts



Sasquatch

The most famous account of Sasquatch was the "Patterson-Gimlin Film" incident. The incident

took place in Northern California. Two filmmakers, Roger Patterson and Bob Gimlin were riding

on horseback when they came upon a large creature covered in hair. Patterson pulled his camera

and the result was the famous Patterson-Gimlin Film, a film still which can be seen on the Database

of this website.



The Mothman

The most famous account of Mothman is also he most tragic account of any cryptid. Residents of

Point Pleasant, WV had been reporting sightings of Mothman for months. The final sighting of the Mothman

happened over the Silver Bridge of Point Pleasant. The next day on December 15, 1967, the bridge collapsed

killing 42 people.
