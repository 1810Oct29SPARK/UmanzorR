  • Chupacabra
    • Spanish for the "goat-sucker, usually described as a 3 foot
    reptilian like quadrepedal creature. Usually, foound in Central
    and South America, Puerto Rico, Mexico and the Southern USA.
  • Mothman
    • A tall gray man, with large ten foot wings. Said
    to be most commonly found at locations right before tragedy strikes,
    most famously at the sight of the Silver Bridge collapse.
  • Champ
    • A terrifying sea monster located in Lake Champlain, where
    its name derives from. Native Americans call Champ "Tatoskok".
  • Wendigo
    • A man eating creature, described as large and emaciated, with
    grotesque antlers. Known as the embodiment of gluttony by the Native
    Americans of the Great Lake region.
  • Loveland Frog
    • Described as a 4ft tall, bipedal frog. First spotted in Loveland,
    Ohio, the city where it gets its name from. Also described as a large
    iguana on two legs.
  • Melon Heads
    • Short humanoid creatures, wuth large bulbous heads that attack people
    in groups at night. Said to be the result of an evil experiment by a mad
    scientist.
  • The Goatman
    • A half-goat, half-man humanoid with the head and legs of a goat and the
    torso of a human. Sightings of him usually come with descreption of him holding
    an axe.
  • The Jersey Devil
    • A large, bipedal horse with wings. Common descriptions involve it having
    the body of a kangaroo,the head of a horse, and a forked tail. It has been reported
    to move quickly and to let out a blood-curdling scream.
  • Jackalope
    • Reported sightings describe this creature as a jackrabbit with antelope horns.
    There is actual debate on whether sightings of these creature were jackrabbits with
    tumors on their head or some imaginative taxidermist's.
  • Honey Island Swamp Mmonster
    • The creature is described as a 7ft tall bipedal; it has gray hair and yellow eyes
    , and is usually accompanied with a horrid smell.
