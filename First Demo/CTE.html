CTE: What is it?

CTE, also know as Chronic Traumatic Encephelopathy is a degenerative neurological diorder that is commonly found

found in people who participate in sports that involve repeated blows to the head; be it Boxing, Wrestling, Football, etc.


Symptoms

The Symptoms of CTE involve

  1. Depression
  2. Mood Swings
  3. Emotional Instability
  4. Impulsiveness
  5. Scattered Thoughts
  6. Memory Loss

On occassion, these symptoms lead to agressive and sometimes violent acts.


Diagnosis

CTE is diagnosed by examining the brain tissue of a person that is suspected of having it. So far the only way to do this is to wait for

for a post-mortem biopsy for a diagnosis. Researchers continue to work on ways to get a diagnosis during a patients life.
